Environmental Impact of Meat, Dairy and Plant Based Diets

When thinking about the environmental impact of different types of meat, it is important we think beyond just carbon emissions. In addition to green house gas emissions stemming from livestock and the animal agriculture industry as a whole, we need to also consider land use. Livestock covers roughly 45% of the earth's total land, it is highly plausible that more efficient use of land would allow for potential reforestation and implementation of carbon sequestration.

In this section of the website, we proceed to understand the environmental impact of beef, poultry, pork, and dairy.

Beef

Greenhouse Gas Emissions (kg CO2 equivalent per 1000 dietary calories)

9.6

Land Use (Square meters used to produce 1000 dietary calories)

146.6

Water Use (Liters used per 1000 dietary calories)

1642

• Beef requires 28 times the land and 11 times the irrigation water as compared to protein calories sourced from dairy, poultry and pork. Beef also results in 5 times the production of greenhouse gases, and puts 6 times the average reactive nitrogen load into the environment. (ESHEL 2014)
• Beef production contributes 2.9 gigatonnes CO2 equivalent, 40% of the livestock sector's total emissions1.
• Enteric fermentation, the cow's digestive process, represents 43% of total emissions in beef supply chains1.
• Beef production alone contributes to around 4% of total US greenhouse gas emissions (ESHEL 2016) • Within current US food system, beef cattle consume 21% of grain and soy concentrates, 92% of pasture, and 87% of processed roughage3 (ESHEL 2015).
• Beef alone accounts for ~88% of all U.S. land used to produce animal-based calories • 2500 gallons of water are needed to produce 1 pound of beef2.
• Beef cattle are inefficient at converting feed into muscle and fat. Thus, the large quantity of crops needed to feed cattle makes beef the most water intensive livestock category.

Poultry

Greenhouse Gas Emissions (kg CO2 equivalent per 1000 dietary calories)

1.7

Land Use (Square meters used to produce 1000 dietary calories)

3.1

Water Use (Liters used per 1000 dietary calories)

145

• Of beef, pork and poultry (chicken), poultry has the lowest feed conversion ratio, amount of feed necessary to produce 1 kg of meat.
• As a result of this and lower methane emissions, poultry is more sustainable to consume than pork or cattle. • Greenhouse gas emissions from the poultry industry is primariy from feed supply and the utilization of fossil fuels for mobile (trucks and tractors) or stationary (furnaces or incinerators) combustion units.
• Poultry are monogastric animals with a simple stomach where little microbial fermentation takes place. As a result, they rpoduce significanlty less methane than cattle (polygastric with a four compartment stomach) 4.
• Poultry is responsible for ~7.2 million tonnes of nutrogen fertilizer use to feed production. • Poultry uses significantly less land than beef. This is largely due to Poultry's lower feed conversion ratio. Chickens need to consume 2 kg of grain to produce 1 kg of meat, beef need 10 6.
• Water use of poultry is primarily determined by feed conversion efficiency.
• Feed conversion is lowest for industrial farms and is followed by mixed systems. Grazying farms are the least efficient type of feed conversion system.

Pig

Greenhouse Gas Emissions (kg CO2 equivalent per 1000 dietary calories)

2

Land Use (Square meters used to produce 1000 dietary calories)

5.3

Water Use (Liters used per 1000 dietary calories)

187

• Pork is a more sustainable protein than beef. This is due to a lower feed conversion ratio and also because pigs do not have a rumen and thus produce less methane.
• In the pork supply chain, the bulk of greenhouse gas emissions are related to feed supply and manure storage in processing 7.
• Feed production is the largest contributor to land use in the pork production process. Pork is less efficient at converting feed than chickens, but more efficient than beef. Pigs need to consume 4 kg of grain to produce 1 kg of meat, beef need 10.
• In a conventional pork productino facility, the average area needed per pig throughout the production phase os ~2.4m2 8.
• Poultry uses a comparable amount of water as chicken

Dairy

Greenhouse Gas Emissions (kg CO2 equivalent per 1000 dietary calories)

1.9

Land Use (Square meters used to produce 1000 dietary calories)

8.7

Water Use (Liters used per 1000 dietary calories)

171

• Dairy is more similar to poultry or pork in terns of greenhouse gas emissions than beef cattle.
• Dairy cows consume a greater proportion of concentrated feed while beef cattle consume a larger share of pasture and processed roughage (hay and silage). This disceprancy means that dairy cows place less of a burden on the land.
• 1000 gallons of water are required to produce 1 gallon of milk, and almost 900 gallons of water are needed for 1lb of cheese 9 9.

Plant Based Diets

Greenhouse Gas Emissions (kg CO2 equivalent per 1000 dietary calories)

0.3

Land Use (Square meters used to produce 1000 dietary calories)

1.7

Water Use (Liters used per 1000 dietary calories)

50

• From an environmental standpoint, a vegan diet or diet primarily consisting of plant-based proteins is much more sustainable and significantly reduces your diet's impact on greenhouse gas emissions, land use and water use.
• A person who follows a vegan diet produces the equivalent of 50% less carbon dioxide, 1/13th water, and 1/18th land compared to a meat-lover for their food 10.
• Each day, a person who eats a vegan diet saves 1100 gallons of water, 45 pounds of grain, 20 lbs of carbon dioxide equivalent 10.
